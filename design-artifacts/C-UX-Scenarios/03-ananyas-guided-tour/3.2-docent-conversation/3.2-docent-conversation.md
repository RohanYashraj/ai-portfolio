## Overview

**Page Purpose:** The first exhibit proves itself. The Agno docent answers real questions strictly from CMS content, suggests next taps, takes messages, and fails gracefully. **Storyboard format** — conversation states matter more than layout.

**User Situation:** Ananya opened it from About the Artist's suggested question — her real questions (exam path, first AI project, where to start) finally have somewhere to go. Elena and Rahul use it transactionally; Ananya conversationally.

**Success Criteria:** 30% of visitors interact (Objective 3); answers grounded in CMS with pointers; message capture works; site remains fully excellent with the agent off.

**Entry Points:**
- DocentLauncher chip in any room (each pre-seeds room context) · About's suggested question · The Study's docent desk (opens in message state)

**Exit Points:**
- Inline pointers → any room/case (panel closes or minimizes) · message sent → confirmation · close (✕/swipe-down) returns to page untouched

## Storyboard: Conversation States

### Frame 1 — Greeting

**When:** Panel opens (chip or launcher)
**Docent:** Pre-contextualized opener. From About: "You were reading Rohan's story — happy to fill in the gaps. What would you like to know?"
**Chips:** "How did he go from exams to AI?" · "What should I read first?" · "Leave a message"
**Rule:** Never opens unprompted; never modal-on-load.

### Frame 2 — Grounded Answer

**When:** Question within CMS knowledge
**Docent:** Concise answer + inline pointers: "…his first applied project was claims triage with LLMs → [see the case]. For a starting point, he suggests the 2024 pricing-ML paper → [in the Archive]."
**Chips:** Follow-ups + "Show me in the gallery"
**Rule:** Every factual claim traceable to CMS content; pointers deep-link.

### Frame 3 — Boundary (honest miss)

**When:** Question outside CMS knowledge
**Docent:** "That's not in my gallery notes — I only speak from what's on record here. Want me to pass the question to Rohan directly?"
**Chips:** "Yes, pass it on" (→ Frame 4) · "Back to his work"
**Rule:** Never improvise; the honest miss IS the credibility feature.

### Frame 4 — Message-Taking

**When:** User opts to leave a message (any frame, or Study entry)
**Docent:** Guided capture in-conversation: "I'll take it down. Your name?" → "Where can Rohan reach you? (email, optional)" → message confirmed back to user before sending
**Rule:** Conversational, not a form-in-a-chat; optional email respected.

### Frame 5 — Confirmation

**When:** Message stored + notification sent
**Docent:** "It's on Rohan's desk — he typically replies within two working days. Anything else while you're here?"
**Chips:** Room suggestions
**Rule:** Same response promise as The Study (one truth).

### Frame 6 — Fallback (agent off/unreachable)

**When:** Agno service disabled or erroring
**Launcher:** Hidden on failure detected at load; mid-conversation error → docent bubble: "I've lost my notes for a moment. You can reach Rohan directly at The Study → or {email}."
**Rule:** The site is fully excellent with the docent off — no broken affordances anywhere.

## Technical Notes

- Agno agent, knowledge = CMS content only (same source as pages); retrieval scoped, no open-web
- Room context passed on open (`page`, `nearestExhibit`) for pre-seeding
- Messages land in the same inbox as Study form submissions
- Engagement analytics: open rate, first-tap rate, message conversions (Objective 3: 30%)
- Rate-limit + abuse guard on input; no PII retention beyond message capture
