## Project Scope

A complete redesign and replacement of Dr. Rohan Yashraj Gupta's personal portfolio website, **rohanyashraj.com**.

The new site is a **"gallery retrospective"** experience — *"Rohan Gupta: Selected Works"* — structured as a six-room sequence:

1. **Entrance** — name + niche line: *"Dr. Rohan Yashraj Gupta — the AI Actuary"*, anchored by "actuary (FIA, FIAI) × data scientist"
2. **Selected Works** — recent highlights, placard-style cards, shown before anything else
3. **About the Artist** — short human story + one portrait
4. **The Archive** — all projects, papers, and talks; filterable, one tap deep
5. **Speaking & Writing** — conference presentations, publications, and a quiet "Invite Rohan to speak" path
6. **The Study** — contact form + the docent's desk

An embedded **AI docent agent (built with the Agno framework)** accompanies visitors through every room — it can explain any highlight, answer questions about Rohan, and take a message. All content (pages *and* agent knowledge) is single-sourced from a CMS.

Content migrates from the existing site: intro/about, resume, projects, conference presentations, research publications, contact.

**Platform:** Web, mobile-first.

---

## Challenge / Opportunity

The current rohanyashraj.com is a clean but conventional resume-style page that undersells Rohan's professional stature and is not effortless to update.

The opportunity: carve out and **own the "AI Actuary" niche** — a distinctive positioning at the intersection of actuarial science and AI. The site must *demonstrate* AI fluency rather than claim it (the docent agent is itself the proof — the first exhibit in the gallery), positioning Rohan for speaking engagements, consulting, and career opportunities. Ambition: the term "AI Actuary" should search to him.

---

## Design Goals

- **Prove competence in 15 seconds** — highlights-first; work before words; content visible in under a second
- **Distinctive, non-template design** — gallery aesthetic (generous whitespace, few pieces given big space, placard captions) with a dash of magazine editorial typography
- **Interactive but effortless** — the docent carries the interactivity; suggested taps keep cognitive load near zero
- **"Delight in the details, never in the way"** — placard captions that type in on scroll, subtle count-up stats, gallery-lighting dark mode (polish candidates, not committed scope)
- **Unambiguously a portfolio** — the purpose of the site is never in doubt
- Explicit anti-goals: no skill-logo walls (skills appear only inside work), no buzzword bio, no carousels or intro spinners, no dead/unmaintained sections, one great portrait used once

---

## Constraints

- **Mobile-friendly is non-negotiable**; animations degrade gracefully on low-power devices (test on mid-range Android, not just iPhone)
- **CMS as single source of truth** (Sanity-style; exact platform TBD) feeding both pages and the agent — effortless updatability is an irreducible requirement
- **Agent resilience:** the site must be fully excellent with the agent turned off; the agent answers only from CMS content with graceful fallback to email
- **Contact form** with honeypot anti-spam; the agent doubles as an alternate contact path
- **No dead sections:** a blog-like "Notes" wing is deferred until content exists
- Timeline / budget: flexible
- Reference input: content and information architecture from the existing site ([github.com/RohanYashraj/rohanyashraj.github.io](https://github.com/RohanYashraj/rohanyashraj.github.io))
